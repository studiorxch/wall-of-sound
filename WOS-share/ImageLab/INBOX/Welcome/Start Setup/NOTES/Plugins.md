# 🌐 What Actually Exists in the Real World

You aren't crazy for thinking about this—the software world has been chasing this exact idea for the last few years. If you want to see how other people are trying to turn note-taking from passive storage into proactive "8-balls," look at these three real products:

- **InfraNodus**: This is the closest thing on Earth to your idea. You type your diary entries or thoughts into it, and it instantly generates a network graph. Instead of just showing you the graph, its algorithm identifies the "structural gaps" (the missing pieces) and literally generates a text prompt on your screen saying: _"You talk about X and Y, but there is a gap between them. What is the link?"_
- **Heptabase**: A visual note-taking tool built entirely around the concept of mind-mapping and canvases. It doesn’t use automated math to find gaps, but it is built for people who need to physically lay out cards visually to see how things connect.
- **Reflect Notes**: A diary app that integrates AI. It reads your daily notes and proactively surfaces old notes from months ago, asking you if your current thought is related to that past event.
