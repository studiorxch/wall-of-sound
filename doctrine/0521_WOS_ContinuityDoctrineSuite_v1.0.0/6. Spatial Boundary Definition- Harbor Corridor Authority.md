

### 6.1 Geographic Continuity Bounds

The Harbor Corridor Authority (HCA) regulates the transition zone where localized urban routes intersect with deep-water channels and low-altitude flight paths.

- **The Boundary:** The spatial boundary is defined as a dynamic polygon starting **300 meters inland from the physical coastline/bulkhead line** and extending outward across the open water.
    
- **The Logic:** This overlap ensures that when a `Vessel` approaches a slip, or a `TransitLine` hits a water-bound terminal, both the urban layout logic and the maritime telemetry logic co-exist within a shared buffer zone before the system falls completely dormant or switches tracking authority.
    

---

### 6.2 The Atlantic Vector

The Atlantic Vector handles long-duration, macro-scale dead reckoning for entities entering or leaving the planetary tracking scale.

- **The Vector Path:** Extends from the inner harbor mouth (The Narrows) out past the ambient visual clipping plane into the open Atlantic shelf.
    
- **Tracking Shift:** Once an asset hits the Atlantic Vector, its runtime footprint transitions fully into the **Low-Frequency Tick** profile ($0.1\text{Hz}$ or sub-sampled background calculations). It ceases to scale its movement based on local pixels per second and instead shifts to wide-coordinate interpolation. It is no longer a localized actor; it is a silent, global macro-signal.
    

---

### 6.3 Airspace Implications

The HCA vertical volume defines how flight corridors overlay the geographic truth of the map.

- **Layered Ceilings:** Airspace is treated as a stacked structural hierarchy directly above the 2D map truth:
    
    - **Low-Altitude Drone/Transit Corridors:** Directly bound to urban street grid geography. They inherit the active _Expressive Grammar_ of the districts they pass over.
        
    - **High-Altitude HCA Flight Paths:** Completely decoupled from the street grid. They ignore local topology and slice diagonally across the map along linear vectors, governed entirely by wide-scale broadcast pacing. When an aircraft is in this layer, it uses the **Visibility Gating** strategy—simulating its path continuously across the canvas even if the camera is tightly locked on a single Brooklyn intersection below.
        

---

### 6.4 City Edge Fade Behavior

How the rendering engine (`wall/render/overlays`) handles the visual death of the city grid as it meets the water.

- **The Mechanism:** The sharp, high-contrast vector lines of the street grid must not abruptly terminate at the water's edge. Instead, they enforce a **Procedural Falloff Haze**.
    
- **The Style:** As coordinates approach the HCA line, line widths thin down, path opacity steps down from `1.00` toward `0.15`, and color values shift from high-contrast accent tones into the muted background palette of the _Overcast_ atmosphere. The city layout literally bleeds out into the dark water, leaving only the primary shipping channels and harbor radar anchors illuminated.
