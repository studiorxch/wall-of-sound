
### 4.1 The Doctrine of Atmospheric Retrospection

The camera is not an entity tracking target data points; it is **observability infrastructure** responsible for interpreting _environmental continuity_.

When a primitive or a specific geographical corridor transitions into a _Dormant State Profile_, it must not be instantly stripped of its **Camera Authority**. If the camera immediately snaps away the moment a `Vessel` anchors or a traffic pipeline suspends, it shatters the psychological weight of the world.

WOS camera systems enforce **Memory Weighting**. The camera remembers where activity _just_ was, allowing the frame to linger, drift, and ease out of a location rather than cutting away hyperactively.

Plaintext

```
[ RUNTIME GATE ] ──────► [ CAMERA AUTHORITY PROFILE ]
Active State             Primary Focus (High Tracking Weight)
▲
│ Transition Phase       Memory Weighting (Lingering Cinematic Drift)
▼
Dormant State            Passive Geometry Anchor (Muted Survey Cadence)
```

### 4.2 Camera Authority Decay Matrix

When a primitive hits a runtime gate, its positional authority in the `BroadcastScheduler`'s tracking matrix decays across three distinct phases:

|**Phase**|**Duration**|**Camera Behavior**|**Framing Philosophy**|
|---|---|---|---|
|**1. Retrospective Ease**|8 to 16 Bars|Retains **High** weight. The camera slows its tracking velocity to a dead stop or a gentle glide, lingering on the newly dormant spatial anchor.|Capturing the echo of movement. Allowing the trailing wake or cooling emission to dissolve naturally within the frame.|
|**2. Cinematic Drift**|32+ Bars|Decays to **Low** weight. The camera detaches its tight framing lock and begins an autonomous, inertial drift along the local `Route` or underlying geographic grid.|Shifting focus from the _actor_ to the _environment_. The camera lets the landscape, the weather, and the structural background layout swallow the asset.|
|**3. Static Substrate Anchor**|Permanent (until woken)|Decays to **Baseline Residual** weight. The camera treats the primitive identically to static infrastructure (buildings, topology). It will only scan it during low-frequency, wide-angle atmospheric surveys.|Treating the dormant system as a landmark. It is part of the persistent map truth, but it no longer commands active broadcast attention.|

### 4.3 Attention Geography Rules

To implement this philosophy across your `wall/render/overlays` and camera sub-systems, three core rules dictate how the lens navigates isolated zones:

#### 4.3.1 The Low-Density Linger

If a camera cuts to an empty corridor where a system _recently_ became dormant, the camera must prioritize wide-angle, slow-pacing frames. It should use the _Atmosphere Doctrine_ (thickening the overcast haze or fog rendering) to lean into the isolation, transforming a lack of runtime data into a deliberate cinematic moment of **structural stillness**.

#### 4.3.2 Shared Geographic Intersection

If an active primitive passes near an isolated, dormant primitive, the dormant primitive's residual memory weight triggers a slight framing bias. The camera will subtly widen its composition to frame _both_ the active asset and the dark ghost of the dormant asset in a single symbolic composition. This connects the living infrastructure to the resting infrastructure.

#### 4.3.3 The Jump-Cut Intercept

The camera is strictly forbidden from jump-cutting directly from a high-velocity active system to a long-dead dormant system. If the scheduler forces a transition to an isolated dormant zone, it must execute a multi-stage camera sequence: fade to a wide environmental baseline overview, establish the geographic landscape, and then ease into the specific dormant corridor.
