# 🧱 Wall of Sound (WOS)

Wall of Sound (WOS) is a persistent audiovisual world broadcasting system where geography, atmosphere, infrastructure, motion, and sound continuously interact.

WOS treats the world itself as a living musical substrate.

Rather than composing on a fixed timeline, WOS generates evolving audiovisual continuity through:

- geography
- telemetry
- infrastructure
- atmosphere
- movement
- observability
- environmental systems
- cinematic interpretation
- procedural continuity

WOS is not designed as a traditional game engine.

It is evolving toward:

```text
persistent programmable world broadcasting infrastructure
```

where viewers occupy living atmospheric worlds rather than simply watching content.

---

# 🎯 Core Concept

WOS replaces traditional linear sequencing with:

```text
WORLD → EVENT → SOUND → WORLD RESPONSE
```

Objects move through environments.
Interactions generate events.
Events trigger sound.
Sound reshapes the world.

The result is:

- emergent music
- atmospheric continuity
- procedural cinema
- persistent observability
- cinematic traversal
- reactive environments
- living infrastructural worlds

WOS treats music as:

```text
environmental continuity
```

rather than fixed arrangement.
