# Edward M. Zajda

## Summary
American electronic composer (born 1941, Chicago) associated with the first generation of independent American electronic music. Trained as a traditional composer before building one of Chicago's earliest private electronic music studios and later adopting the Moog synthesizer in the late 1960s.

## Why This Belongs in the Archive
Zajda represents the transition from self-built electronic studios to early commercial synthesizer practice. His work is valuable less as a canonical artist than as evidence of independent, infrastructure-driven experimentation outside the major academic electronic music centers.
