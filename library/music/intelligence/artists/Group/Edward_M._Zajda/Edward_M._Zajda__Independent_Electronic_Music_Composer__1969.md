# Independent Electronic Music Composer (1969)

## Summary
An early independent American electronic music album documenting Edward M. Zajda's explorations with tape composition and early Moog synthesizer techniques. Rather than functioning as popular electronic music, the album documents a period when electronic composition was still largely an experimental studio practice.

## Why This Belongs in the Archive
This album captures electronic music as infrastructure and process. Its value lies in exposing compositional mechanisms, analog workflows, and self-directed experimentation that predate later electronic genres.
