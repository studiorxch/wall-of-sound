# Bonobo

## Summary

Bonobo (Simon Green) is a significant StudioRich reference for environmental composition, emotional geography, and organic-electronic integration. His work builds immersive spaces through layered instrumentation, field recordings, rhythm, and atmosphere, creating environments that feel both physical and dreamlike.

## Why This Belongs in the Archive

Bonobo demonstrates how worldbuilding can emerge from place, texture, movement, and mood rather than narrative. His music functions as environmental architecture, constructing landscapes that suggest travel, memory, observation, and connection. The project provides reusable intelligence for designing atmospheric systems that feel global, human, and spatially rich.

## Key Eras

### Early Downtempo Era (1999–2005)

Sample-based compositions rooted in downtempo, trip-hop, and organic instrumentation.

### Expanding Horizons Era (2006–2012)

Greater emphasis on live musicianship, world influences, and environmental storytelling.

### Cinematic Systems Era (2013–2019)

Large-scale compositions focused on movement, emotional geography, and audiovisual immersion.

### Global Atmosphere Era (2020–Present)

Refined integration of electronic systems, live instrumentation, and place-based sonic identity.
