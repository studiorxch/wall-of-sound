# Richie Hawtin

## Summary

Richie Hawtin is a foundational StudioRich reference for systems thinking, reduction, and technological worldbuilding. Across his solo work, Plastikman, live performance practice, and label building, he treats music as an evolving system where interfaces, software, hardware, and human interaction become compositional tools.

## Why This Belongs in the Archive

Hawtin demonstrates how technical infrastructure can become cultural infrastructure. His work extends beyond records into performance design, workflow innovation, audiovisual identity, and networked creative ecosystems, making him one of the strongest references for technology-driven worldbuilding.

## Key Eras

### Plus 8 Foundations (1989–1993)

Detroit-influenced techno focused on machine rhythm and functional dance-floor systems.

### Plastikman Era (1993–2005)

Minimalism, psychoacoustic space, repetition, and a complete audiovisual identity system.

### M_nus Systems Era (1998–2010)

Expansion into label architecture, artist networks, workflow experimentation, and minimalist ecosystems.

### Interface & Network Era (2010–Present)

Focus on live technology, software integration, immersive audiovisual performance, and digital creative infrastructure.
