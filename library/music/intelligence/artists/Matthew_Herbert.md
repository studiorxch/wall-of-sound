# Matthew Herbert

## Summary

Matthew Herbert is one of the strongest StudioRich references for treating sound as a documentation system. His work transforms everyday objects, environments, politics, and human activity into musical structure through rigorous conceptual rules and inventive sampling.

## Why This Belongs in the Archive

Herbert demonstrates that worldbuilding can begin with ordinary reality rather than fictional settings. By assigning compositional rules to real-world materials—food, machines, bodies, public spaces, or environmental recordings—he converts observation into reusable creative systems.

## Key Eras

### Herbert & Doctor Rockit

Experimental house built from unconventional sound sources and playful sampling.

### Personal Signature

Development of a strict manifesto-driven approach where compositional rules become part of the artwork.

### Orchestral & Documentary Work

Expansion into large ensembles, film scoring, opera, and socially engaged sound projects.

### Environmental Effect

Creates environments that feel like kitchens, factories, cities, markets, and public spaces reimagined as living musical systems.

## Visual / Digital Profile

A restrained visual identity that places emphasis on process and ideas rather than branding, reinforcing the documentary and investigative nature of the work.
